Chance constrained reservoir risk allocation

The aim of this research is to predict the optimal cost and allocate appropriate risks for reservoir in a multi-reservoir system. This is carried out by using Stochastic optimization methods and Chance constrained programming. The code is written in MATLAB.

Automating airfoil analysis to identify flow separation and implement control techniques

I worked at the Thermal Turbomachines Lab in IIT Madras under Prof. N. Sitaram (now retired) as a part of my honors thesis and was co-advised by Dr. N. Jalaiah.
I automated an entire airfoil analysis with the help of Python and ANSYS FLUENT which is now being used in the lab for simulations. The combined work of me and my teammate yielded a few publications.
Softwares used: ANSYS, FLUENT, ICEM CFD and Python

CanSat Competition 2013

I led the first ever team from any of the BITS campuses to participate in the CanSat Competition conducted annually by the AAS and AIAA.
The aim was to build a "Can Satellite" that mimics the functions of a normal satellite such as telemetry, aero-braking system, etc. I raised an amount of $1,500 as sponsorship and we successfully launched our CanSat, Varuna, from Abilene, Texas on the summer of 2013.
Subsequently, the design and concept was presented in front of the Director of ISRO and has paved way for participations in the future events.

Flow separation over a NACA4421 airfoil

This was a term project along with a temporary faculty at BITS to understand the nature of flows over airfoils. I modelled a NACA4421 airfoil and simulated the flow over angles of attack varying from 0 to 30 degrees to identify the most effective point of flow control.
This was further developed during my undergraduate thesis to establish passive flow control techniques.
Technologies used:ANSYS FLUENT, OpenFOAM, C

Vibrational analysis of human body in motion

As a sophomore, I interned at the Indian Institute of Science under Prof. Omkar and also guided by Prof. S.B. Kandagal on certain technical fields. I created a lumped parameter model of the human body with varying degrees of freedom to test the accuracy of each model for the specific case of aircraft seat ejection. The vibrational analysis was carried out using the concept of state-space models.
Softwares used:MATLAB, SimuLink
